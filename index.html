About Me

Harshith VH
Student · Researcher

I'm a final-year BSc Physics & Computer Science student at Kristu Jayanti (Deemed to be University), Bengaluru, working at the intersection of astrophysics and computational science. My research spans gravitational wave data analysis, exoplanet imaging, and cosmic ray physics.

In June 2025, I completed a Research Internship at TIFR's Cosmic Ray Laboratory, Ooty — working hands-on with the Muon Telescope setup to study secondary cosmic ray muon flux, operating scintillator-based detectors, and performing data acquisition and preliminary analysis in a real experimental astrophysics environment.

Beyond the lab, I build LLM agents, full-stack web applications, develop machine learning pipelines for scientific data, and conduct workshops on gravitational wave analysis and exoplanet transit curves for physics students. I believe the most powerful tools in modern astrophysics live at the boundary of physics and code.

Evolving Sensitivity of LIGO Livingston: Inspiral Range and Mass Scaling Across Observing Runs
Analyzed LIGO Livingston detector sensitivity across O1, O2, and O3 using ASD and noise characterization. Computed inspiral ranges for BNS, BBH, and BHNS systems showing significant sensitivity improvement (BNS: ~63 Mpc → ~110 Mpc). Identified deviation from theoretical d∝M^(5/6).

Q-Transform Analysis of GW230529
Performed Q-transform time-frequency analysis of GW230529, a suspected neutron star–black hole (NSBH) merger. Identified a significant signal between −9 and −9.2 s relative to trigger time, suggesting potential early inspiral or noise artefacts.

05 — Projects

Projects & Work

CMB Foreground Separtion using PCA & ICA
The Cosmic Microwave Background is the oldest electromagnetic signal in the universe — but observing it cleanly is impossible due to galactic foregrounds (synchrotron radiation and thermal dust) that overwhelm the faint CMB signal across microwave frequencies. This project simulates a realistic multi-frequency flat-sky CMB observation and applies two machine learning component separation algorithms — **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)** and **Independent Component Analysis (ICA)** — to blindly recover the primordial CMB map.
ML Numpy CMB EDA
